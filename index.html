Global Data on Sustainable Energy (2000-2020)

The primary focus of this visualization will be on showcasing sustainable energy indicators from across all countries such as electricity access, renewable energy, carbon emissions, and energy intensity. Giving a holistic view into global energy consumption patterns over time.

CO2 Emissions

For the majority of countries, their CO2 emissions have risen over time. There also seems to be a slight correlation between Energy Consumption per capita as well as the CO2 emissions.

The three key countries which had been identified are: China which exponentially grew to emitthe most CO2. Qatar which consumed the most energy per capita. Finally United States, which emitted the mos CO2 up until the year 2005

Access to energy and use of electricity

Over time, all countries around the world have gained more access to electricity. Especially in areas such as Africa, and Middle East Asia.

Surprisingly over time the renewable share of energy didn't seem to increase as much throughout the globe. With Africa having the highest usage of renewable energy.

Sources of Electricity

Fossil Fuels have consistently been the highest source of electricity around the globe. With many continents choosing to utilise it more over time.

Although this can potentially be balanced out by the increase in use of Renewable energy over time as well.
